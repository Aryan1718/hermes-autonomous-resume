# Three-Layer Read — Detailed Methodology

Read the JD three times, each pass looking for different things. Do not try to do all three at once.

## Pass 1: Surface (Hard Requirements)

**What to look for:**
- Tools, software, platforms (e.g., Python, Salesforce, Figma)
- Certifications (e.g., PMP, AWS Certified)
- Years of experience required
- Degrees or formal qualifications
- Specific methodologies (Agile, Six Sigma, etc.)

**How to capture:** Make a bulleted list. These are non-negotiable for ATS systems.

## Pass 2: Behavioral (How They Work)

**What to look for:**
- Action verbs: "drive," "own," "execute," "collaborate," "influence," "build"
- Working style cues: "fast-paced," "ambiguous," "structured," "highly collaborative"
- Decision-making language: "data-driven," "intuitive," "consensus-building"

**Why it matters:** These verbs reveal *how* the company expects work to get done. A company that says "drive" wants initiators. A company that says "support" wants reliable executors. Use their verbs in your bullets.

**Example:**
- JD says: *"Drive product strategy and own outcomes end-to-end."*
- Signal: They want a high-agency operator, not a coordinator. Resume bullets should start with "Drove," "Owned," "Led."

## Pass 3: Cultural/Intent (The Real Why)

**What to look for:**
- What problem is this role solving?
- What is broken, missing, or growing?
- What does success look like in 6–12 months (if stated)?

**Example:**
- JD says: *"We're looking for someone to build our analytics function from the ground up."*
- Intent: They have no analytics today. They want a builder, not a maintainer. Emphasize 0→1 projects.
