# Must-Haves vs. Nice-to-Haves vs. Filler — Methodology

## Bucket 1: Non-Negotiables (Must-Haves)

**How to identify:**
- Mentioned 2+ times in the JD
- Appear in the job title
- Listed under "minimum qualifications" or "required"

**Rule:** These MUST appear on the resume, ideally in the **top third** of the document (summary, top role bullets, or skills section).

## Bucket 2: Preferred (Nice-to-Haves)

**How to identify:**
- Mentioned once
- Under "preferred," "bonus," or "nice to have"

**Rule:** Include if you genuinely have them. Skip if you don't — do not fake.

## Bucket 3: Filler

**How to identify:**
- Generic corporate language: "team player," "passionate," "fast-paced environment"
- HR-mandated boilerplate

**Rule:** Ignore for keyword extraction. But **mirror the tone** — if the JD is formal, your resume should be formal; if it's casual, loosen up.

## Example

**JD says:**
> "We're seeking a Senior Data Analyst with 5+ years of SQL experience. Experience with Python is required. Familiarity with dbt is a plus. Must be a self-starter who thrives in a fast-paced environment."

**Categorization:**
- **Must-haves:** SQL (5+ years), Python, Senior Data Analyst title-equivalent experience
- **Preferred:** dbt
- **Filler:** "self-starter," "fast-paced environment" (tone signal only)
